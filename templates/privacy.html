Online Privacy

Understanding how to protect your personal information online is essential in today's digital world.

What is Online Privacy?

Online privacy refers to the level of protection for your personal data when you're using the internet. It includes how data is collected, stored, shared, and used by websites, applications, and other online services.

Did you know? The average person has their data collected by 12 different companies every day when browsing online.

Key Privacy Concerns

Data Collection

Websites and applications collect data about your browsing habits, purchases, location, and personal information through cookies, trackers, and sign-up forms.

Targeted Advertising

Your online activity is used to create a profile about you, which is then used to target personalized advertisements to you across different platforms.

Data Brokers

Companies collect, analyze, and sell your personal information to other businesses for marketing purposes, creating detailed profiles about your preferences.

Surveillance

Government agencies and corporations may monitor online activities, messaging, and social media, sometimes without users' knowledge or consent.

How to Protect Your Privacy Online

Consider using browsers that prioritize privacy like Firefox or Brave. Install privacy extensions such as Privacy Badger, uBlock Origin, or HTTPS Everywhere to block trackers and encrypt connections.

Pro Tip: Regularly clear your browser cookies and cache to remove stored tracking data.

Create strong, unique passwords for each of your online accounts. Consider using a password manager to generate and store complex passwords securely.

Good example: "j8K!p2&xLm9@zA" is much stronger than "password123"

A VPN encrypts your internet connection and masks your IP address, making it more difficult for websites, your ISP, or third parties to track your online activities.

Remember: Not all VPNs are created equal. Research before choosing one, as some may collect and sell your data.

Regularly review and adjust privacy settings on social media platforms, Google accounts, mobile apps, and other online services. Limit the data you share and who can access it.

Pro Tip: Set a calendar reminder to review your privacy settings every few months.

Add an extra layer of security to your accounts by enabling 2FA, which requires both your password and a second form of verification (like a code sent to your phone).

Fun fact: 2FA can prevent over 99% of automated account hacking attempts!

Digital Footprint Awareness

Your digital footprint is the trail of data you leave behind when using the internet. It includes:

  • Websites you visit
  • Emails you send
  • Social media activity
  • Online purchases
  • App usage
  • Location data

Be mindful of what you share online, as this information can be compiled to create a detailed profile about you. Regularly search for yourself online to see what information is publicly available.
